Heatwave vulnerability in Paris
MCA project — GIS Level 1
The problem
Planning questionWhere are communities most vulnerable to heatwave mortality across Paris?
Why this topic?Paris was not built to withstand modern temperatures — my family is currently experiencing this firsthand.
Why MCA?
Multiple factors at onceHeat vulnerability is shaped by many overlapping criteria — MCA lets us consider them all simultaneously.
Weighted decision supportEach factor can be given a different importance, reflecting the real-world evidence on heat mortality.

Data sources
OpenStreetMapBase map of Paris; infrastructure; distances from residences to green areas
Satellite imageryMaps NDVI (vegetation) and land surface temperature; shows how much heat different land covers reflect
INSEE censusFrance's national statistics bureau — age, occupation, housing conditions, income by area
GIS workflow
1. Prepare datasetsReproject all layers to the same coordinate system; clip to Paris boundary
›
2. Reclassify criteriaRescale each layer to a 1–5 suitability score so all criteria are comparable
›
3. Apply weightsElderly %, income deprivation, and surface temp receive highest weights
›
4. Combine layersMultiply each raster by its weight; sum all into one output raster
›
5. Final mapCreate vulnerability map ranking Paris areas from low to high risk
Expected output
Vulnerability mapParis arrondissements ranked by combined heat vulnerability score
Decision supportIdentifies priority areas for cooling centres, welfare checks, and emergency response
