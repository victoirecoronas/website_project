Choropleth Map in QGIS

This projects showcases a choropleth map illustrating the proportion of seats held by women in national parliaments across countries using 2025 data and made with QGIS.

United Nations SDGs

This project maps global progress toward the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, focusing on Goal 5: Achieve Gender Equality and Empower All Women and Girls.

The focus is directed toward Target 5.5: "Ensure women's full and effective participation and equal opportunities for leadership at all levels of decision-making." This progress is measured using SDG Indicator 5.5.1, which measures the proportion of seats held by women in national parliaments.

Cartographic Design

Using the data from the United Nations, this choropleth map was built to make patterns identifiable at a quick glance. The key visualization choices I made include:

  • Color Palette: The map uses shades of green because it is a color with positive connotations to represent higher female representation as a positive.
  • Value Steps: Lighter greens illustrate fewer parliamentary seats occupied by women, while increasingly darker shades represent higher proportions.
  • Top Performers: Countries achieving 50% or more female representation are distinguished by a bold outline and explicit text labels showing their percentage data.

The four highest-performing nations highlighted include Rwanda (61.3%), Nicaragua (54.95%), Cuba (53.2%), Mexico (50.2%), and the United Arab Emirates (50.0%).

Reflection & Critique

Choropleth maps are highly effective for visualizing standard percentages and global spatial ratios.

However, a major constraint of this type of map is that a single color usually represents an entire country, masking regional or local variabilities. Furthermore, this dataset features a notable amount of missing data (represented in grey). These information gaps make it challenging to draw complete global comparisons.

Technical Skills Gained

  • Creation of maps on QGIS: I learned how to extract raw datasets and map them effectively. This also highlighted how design choices change what story a map emphasizes and the narrative it choses.
  • Website Development: I gained foundational skills in producing a webiste to display the interative map publicly.
